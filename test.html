Mexico City GP 2025

October 28, 2025

Check out this episode of the Gradient audio podcast.. 

Vamos! 

Round 20 of the 2025 F1 season took us inside to The Autodromo Hermanos Rodriquez for the Grand Prix's 10th Anniversary as the season makes way for the final 5 tracks of year. With a driver's championship battle still up for grabs, midfield teams fighting for millions of dollars in prize and bonus money, and some drivers racing to stay on the grid in 2026. Lots of storylines converged at La Capital so let's run through it. 

The biggest conversation coming into Mexico surrounded Max Verstappen and his historic late season surge to put him in contention to win his 5th title. Coming into this weekend Max Verstappen had won 3 out of the last 4 grand prix (Monza, Baku, and Austin) including a sprint victory where both McLaren drivers DNF after colliding into turn one. In fact since coming out of the summer break in late August the dutchman has yet to not reach the podium in the last 6 races with 3 P1 finshes, 2 P2's and a P3 in Mexico this past weekend (sorry for the spoiler...)

courtesy of formula1points.com

Max is no stranger to making the unthinkable happen, and this year is no exception. After the Dutch GP Max sat 104 points behind the points leader Oscar Piastri. Since that race Verstappen has outscored Oscar Piastri 116:47 and closed the gap to just 36 points the lead with just 4 races to go. 

After Red Bull dismissed long time team principal Christian Horner half way through the year the team all but seemed to be in an identity crisis -  a situation the Milton Keynes based team had yet to face since this current ruleset went into place. Newly Appointed team Principal Laurent Mekkies has done a marvelous job of steering the team back on track as they are eyeing for a hotly contested P2 in the constructors championship with Verstappen closing in on the driver's title as well.

On the other end of the discussion was the concern over Oscar Piastri's recent downturn of performance. How would the Australian driver fair in Mexico City when the pressure of a Verstappen bid has gotten to an uncomfortable distance of becoming a reality. The driver who had been so dominate and consistent weekend to weekend through this long season has had people scratching their heads as of late.
